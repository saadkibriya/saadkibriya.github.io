The Dark Scribe

Dark
Fiction

Before I could write, I was already collecting stories. Not in notebooks — in the dark. Grandmother's voice dropping to a whisper when the story turned. The creak of a wooden house at 2 AM that nobody explained. The feeling that something in the fog outside the window was listening back.

I grew up on Humayun Ahmed's Misir Ali and the quiet dread of Himu wandering Dhaka at night. On Sunday Suspense playing through a phone speaker under the covers. On Tin Goyenda, Feluda, Masud Rana, Bomkesh — every detective who trusted instinct over evidence. On Agatha Christie's impossible murders and Edgar Allan Poe's rooms that breathed. On Lovecraft's monsters that weren't monsters so much as facts the human mind refuses to accept.

I read Harry Potter and then I read everything else. DC and Marvel taught me that origin stories matter — that what breaks you is exactly what makes you. Sherlock Holmes taught me that the thing everyone ignores is usually the most important thing in the room.

By the time I started writing, I wasn't starting — I was translating. The stories had been accumulating for twenty-something years. The horror, the paranormal, the characters who feel slightly wrong in ways you can't immediately name. I write in Bangla and English because some stories sound more dangerous in one language than the other. I'm still figuring out which is which.

Most of what I write starts as a 3 AM thought that seemed unreasonable and turned out to be the whole story.

"The scariest things are the ones that make perfect sense in the morning — and still scare you anyway."

Adding a new story — minimal work, always

When you finish a story: write it in Google Docs or Keep → copy the text → open the story template file (story-template.html, included in your download) → paste the text in the marked sections → rename the file (e.g. story-new.html) → upload to GitHub alongside the others → add a card here linking to it. That's the entire process. No CMS, no backend, no subscriptions.
