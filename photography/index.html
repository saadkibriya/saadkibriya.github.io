Light &
Silence

I didn't start with a camera. I started with a need to remember things before they disappeared — golden hours over the Padma, fog over Bandarban at dawn, the silhouette of a stranger at a bus stop who looked like he was carrying the weight of a whole city.

Since 2019, I've been running @_randomcitizen — no studio, no DSLR, no assistant. Just me, whatever phone I had at the time, and the stubborn belief that the best light shows up when you aren't ready for it. I've gone from a Xiaomi Mi 5 to a Redmi Note 10 Pro Max to a Samsung S23+, and the gear changed but the instinct didn't.

I've travelled across almost all of Bangladesh for these photographs. Chased sunsets in Cox's Bazar, lost myself in the hills of Bandarban, shot the chaos and quiet of Dhaka at 5 AM. Landscapes, rivers, street moments, portraits of people who didn't ask to be photographed but deserved to be seen.

This isn't a portfolio. It's a record of being somewhere and paying attention.
